AI-PUBLIC-INTEREST

What are some of the applications of AI to public-interest?

Jacob Vasquez, Andrew Wun, Aidan McBride

Potential Applications & their implications

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has the power to transform society enhancing healthcare, education, public safety, and access to resources. But who benefits from these technologies? And who bears the risks? When AI systems are developed and deployed primarily for profit or without inclusive design, they can reinforce systemic inequality, threaten privacy, and undermine public trust. As responsible technologists, we must ensure AI serves the broader public interest not just private stakeholders. Using philosopher John Rawls’ concept of the “original position” and the “veil of ignorance,” we can evaluate AI systems by asking: Would I agree to this system if I didn’t know whether I would benefit or be harmed by it? Rawls’ Difference Principle also suggests that inequalities in outcomes (e.g., who benefits from an AI system) are only justified if they improve the position of the least advantaged. Applying this, AI should be developed in a way that prioritizes fairness, accessibility, and accountability. Predictive policing tools use historical crime data to identify potential hotspots or suspects. However, these tools often reflect biased data, leading to over-policing in communities of color. From a Rawlsian perspective, such tools fail the fairness test: if you didn’t know your race or neighborhood, you likely wouldn’t agree to the deployment of a biased system. Reforming or rejecting such tools is necessary when they do not uplift or protect the least advantaged.

AI in Healthcare

For all the concerns to be had with how artificial intelligence will be used in the future, many will find its potential in healthcare and medicine to be very promising. One potential advancement is the ability to assist dermatologists’ ability to make diagnoses from skin imaging tests. Artificial intelligence may become intellgient enough based off increased to diagnose particular issues which may be uncommon but with the datasets implemented may allow for more sufficient decision making. This could be used as an easier and more cost effective way of giving a patient a second opinion, and it may even become strong enough to diagnose patients remotely. This could be really powerful because so many people around the world struggle to see doctors and specialists in a timely manner. Given the power of this technology, it’s important that these advancements support the public good rather than focus on profit. That being said, the overall potential of artificial intelligence in health care is extremely positive.

  • Cost-Effectiveness, implementing AI allows
  • Enhancements from AI allow for stronger patient care
  • Happier Doctor's, more satisfied patients with minimal invasiveness
  • Therefore, Health Care is more Efficient

Ordered List

The implications of AI on Societal services such as healthcare are quite obvious, and astounding. However, the implications in other aspects of life such as law enforcement may bring a bitter sentiment. In the case of law enforcement, many times people's right are infringed upon as is. With the implementation of AI systems, privacy and people's right are at more stake than ever. The worst part, is that it would be a communal burden, with surveillance to feed data to the AI systems who will assist in law enforcement. Now obviously, this brings inherent risk to the well-being of an individual, who'd rather have it otherwise.

  1. Law Enforcement is more efficient
  2. Citizen rights are further infringed upon
  3. More power is given to the few, who monitor these systems
  4. Society is rather dystopian than utopian
