AI Hiring Admissions

Overview

AI in Hiring & Admissions is the use of artificial intelligence technologies to help companies and schools manage the process of selecting applicants. AI is often used to screen resumes, analyze applications, or predict future success based on data patterns. The goal of using AI in these areas is to save time and make faster decisions when dealing with large numbers of candidates. However, using AI in this way raises concerns about fairness, bias, and transparency. It is important to consider how these systems are designed, how their decisions are made, and whether they treat all applicants equally and ethically.

Case Study

HireVue’s “glass box” video interviews are an AI powered, asynchronous screening tool that makes an effort to make automated hiring more transparent and fair. It utilizes a one way video format so candidates can record answers to a set of prerecorded questions and allows employers to be more efficient with their time. HR Dive, the online news network, reported about hard of hearing users having issues with financial software company “Intuit” AI screening tool provided by HireVue. Those users mentioned that the AI generated subtitles were mistranscribing just about every other word and made it harder to follow along which led to them not doing as well in the interview as others who weren’t hard of hearing. HR Dive also mentions that a deaf employee from Intuit requested human generated captions but was denied. She had difficulties understanding the AI generated subtitles in her interview and afterwards she was rejected for a promotion.

Practical Takeaways

When using or designing AI tools in hiring and admissions, it's important to think about fairness, access, and transparency. Below are some tips and questions to keep in mind:

  • Check for Bias: Ask if the AI system has been tested for bias. Does it treat all types of applicants fairly, including people with disabilities or those from different backgrounds?
  • Offer Alternatives: Make sure there are options for people who may have trouble with AI tools (like subtitles for deaf users or human review for unusual cases).
  • Explain Decisions: Can someone clearly explain how the AI made its decision? If not, that’s a red flag.
  • Review Regularly: Don’t set the system and forget it. Keep checking if the AI is making fair decisions as data and society change.
  • Train Humans Too: AI should not replace human judgment. Make sure hiring managers and admissions staff are trained to understand and question AI outcomes.
  • Transparency for Users: Let applicants know when AI is being used and how it affects them. Give them a chance to ask for a different method if needed.

These steps help organizations use AI in a more ethical and responsible way.
